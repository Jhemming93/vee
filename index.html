Housing Reform Goals

Invest in Co-Operative Housing

To partner with people and communities in reforming the relationship between residents and the homes they depend on for well-being

Facilitate formation

Generate resources and partnerships of mutual benefit to support the building of distributed co-operative networks within local areas and aid in funding and ongoing maintenance

Educate, inform, and grow

Promote the development of the distributed co-operative model through education and outreach with the goal of reforming local culture and sustainable housing accessibility

Aspiring Projects

Distributed Co-operatives

Idea: Raise funds to pay off mortgage debt for small groups of existing homeowners who live in a local area. Then establish a financial co-operative structure to share in ongoing home maintenance, development and organization

Goal: Provide framework for an alternative housing "market" and inter-co-operative development

Facilitated Co-operatives

Idea: Raise funds to buy or build multi-family buildings in a local area with no mortgage and facilitate creation of a resident managed financial co-operative structure for the building

Goal: Provide framework for an alternative housing "market" and inter-co-operative development

Artists/Workers Co-operatives

Idea: Raise funds to purchase and reclaim an underutilized building and develop it into combined community art studio and residential apartment units for local artists to produce art, teach workshops and share skills

Goal: A community art and culture hub that allows artists to share resources for living, creating and connecting

Growers/Agri Co-operatives

Idea: Raise funds to purchase and reclaim an underutilized building and develop it into combined hydroponic greenhouse and residential apartment units for locals to grow food, teach workshops and share skills

Goal: A community food and culture hub that allows growers to share resources for living, gardening and connecting
